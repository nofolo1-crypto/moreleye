🍄 Morel Field Guide

Morel Identification

  • Distinctive honeycomb / pitted cap with ridges
  • Fully hollow when sliced vertically — key test
  • Cap is fused to stem at the base
  • Colors: tan, grey, yellow, or black-brown
  • Typical height 2–6 inches; appears in spring
  • Found after rains when soil hits ~50–60°F

Prime Host Trees

  • Dead / dying elm — single best indicator
  • Ash trees killed by Emerald Ash Borer
  • Old apple & other fruit trees / orchards
  • Tulip poplar (yellow poplar)
  • Sycamore and cottonwood near water
  • Mature oak stands (late season)

Habitat Cues

  • South-facing slopes warm up first in spring
  • Floodplains, creek banks, moist hollows
  • Recent logging or fire-disturbed ground
  • Loose, leaf-littered, well-drained soil
  • Look uphill from water sources

⚠ False Morel Warning

  • Gyromitra — brain-like wrinkled cap, NOT pitted
  • Cap is NOT fused to stem — hangs loosely
  • Interior is chambered, not fully hollow
  • Contains gyromitrin — toxic, potentially fatal
  • Always do the hollow slice test before eating
